## Abstract

We investigate the relation between sunspot area and the total unsigned magnetic flux for 33 active regions observed by the Solar Dynamics Observatory's Helioseismic and Magnetic Imager (HMI) between January and November 2025 during the maximum phase of the Solar Cycle 25. Sunspot areas were measured from HMI continuum intensity images using an automated Bradley-Roth adaptive thresholding pipeline with uniform parameters applied across the full sample. Corresponding total unsigned magnetic flux was obtained from the Space-weather HMI Active Region Patch (SHARP) data product. We find a power-law relationship $\Phi = a\,A^{b}$ with $b=1.056 \pm 0.080$ and $R^{2} = 0.857$ consistent within uncertainties with previously published value of $b = 1.010 \pm -.132$ obtained from the Solar Cycles 23-24 using the same instrument. Our results confirm that sunspot area remains a reliable proxy for active-region magnetic flux during an unexpectedly strong cycle maximum, supporting its continued use for rapid space-weather assessment using continuum imaging alone even during high activity levels.
